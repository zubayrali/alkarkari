*Excerpt from a gathering in Fes. Lightly edited for clarity.*

***

**Q: Shaykh, what exactly is the Tariqa Karkariya? Is it a new order or part of something older?**

**A:** The Tariqa is not new. Nothing in it is invented. The \[\[Silsila]] goes back to the Prophet ﷺ, and every practice we do comes from that chain. What is "new," if anything, is the name and the particular form in which this heritage is expressed in our time. A river is always the same river even if it carves a new channel.

**Q: So is it the same as the Alawiyya?**

**A:** The mother is the Alawiyya, and the mother of the Alawiyya is the Shadhiliyya. We do not deny our lineage. But each master receives something and expresses it through his own light. \[\[Ahmad al-Alawi]] expressed the path through his light. What I have been given, I express through mine. The differences are in form, not in essence.

**Q: Who can enter?**

**A:** Anyone who sincerely desires God and is willing to do the work. The door is not closed to anyone — man or woman, scholar or illiterate, Moroccan or European. What matters is the sincerity of the intention and the willingness to be guided.

**Q: What is the first requirement?**

**A:** Tawba. Repentance. Without a genuine turning toward God, nothing that comes after will have roots. We do not build houses in the air.
