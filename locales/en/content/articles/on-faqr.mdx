*From a public lecture, Fes. Translated from Arabic.*

***

People come to the Tariqa for different reasons. Some come because they are suffering and seek relief. Some come because they are curious about the spiritual life. Some come because they heard something beautiful and want more of it. Some come because someone they love is in the path and they followed out of love.

All of these are fine reasons. God uses every door.

But the Tariqa has one destination, and that destination is \[\[Faqr]].

Faqr is not poverty of money. Do not misunderstand me. A rich man can be a faqir. A poor man can be far from faqr. Faqr is poverty of the self before God — the recognition, down to the bone, that you own nothing, that you are nothing without Him, that every breath is a gift, every moment sustained from beyond you.

***

## Why this is not depressing

When people hear "you are nothing," they become sad. But the one who has tasted faqr does not weep over it — he rejoices in it. Because the one who is nothing before God becomes filled with God. The empty cup receives. The full cup — full of itself, full of its own importance — has no room.

The Prophet ﷺ said: "How wonderful is the affair of the believer! All of it is good." This is the experience of the faqir: even difficulty becomes a gift, because even difficulty is the action of the Beloved.

***

## Faqr and the seven pillars

Each of the seven pillars — \[\[Wird]], \[\[Hadra]], \[\[Muraqqa']], \[\[Ism al-Mufrad]], \[\[Siyaha]], \[\[Khalwa]], \[\[Sirr]] — serves faqr. Each one, in its own way, removes something of the ego's pretension. The wird humbles the morning. The hadra shows you that you are not alone. The muraqaba reveals the ego's movements. The Ism al-Mufrad replaces the ego's voice with the divine name. The siyaha loosens the ego's attachment to place. The khalwa strips the ego of social support. And the sirr — the innermost — is where the ego finally becomes transparent.

At the end of all of this is faqr. And faqr, it turns out, is where you always were — you simply did not know it.
