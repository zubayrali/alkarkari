The **Khalwa** is the sixth of the seven pillars. See \[\[Khalwa]] in the Dictionary.

## When khalwa is given

The Shaykh does not prescribe khalwa to every murid. It is given when the murid has demonstrated sufficient stability in the earlier pillars — consistent wird, regular hadra attendance, practising muraqaba — and when the Shaykh discerns that the murid is ready for the intensification that khalwa brings.

Premature khalwa can do harm. The Shaykh is explicit about this.

## What changes in khalwa

Murids who have completed a khalwa describe a qualitative shift in their inner experience — a deepening of the \[\[Muraqqa']] state, a greater ease in the \[\[Ism al-Mufrad]], and often a clarification of their spiritual state and direction.

## Modern khalwa

For murids in Western contexts, the logistics of a forty-day khalwa are challenging. The Shaykh has given guidance on adapted forms — shorter retreats, intensified periods within ordinary life. The principle, not the duration, is what matters.
