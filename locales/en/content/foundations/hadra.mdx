The **Hadra** is the second of the seven pillars of the Tariqa Karkariya. See \[\[Hadra]] in the Dictionary for the full definition.

## The hadra as pillar

Where the wird is the individual's daily anchor, the hadra is the communal heartbeat of the Tariqa. It is the gathering that makes a dispersed community of individuals into a body.

The Shaykh has said that the murid who lives far from a zawiya and cannot attend regular hadras should seek other murids and organize one, even if small. Two or three gathered in remembrance is a hadra.

## The prophetic assembly

The hadra is modeled on the assemblies (*majalis*) of the Prophet ﷺ — gatherings where remembrance, recitation, and presence of the divine were the atmosphere. The companions described these gatherings as "gardens of paradise."

## Obligation and encouragement

The hadra is not obligatory in the same legal sense as prayer, but the Shaykh treats it as close to that in spiritual terms for murids who have access to it. Missing it without reason is considered a weakening of the murid's connection to the community and to the baraka.

```orbit color=blue
Q: What is the role of the hadra among the seven pillars?
A: The communal heartbeat — where the wird is the individual's daily anchor, the hadra makes a dispersed community into a body.

A: Seek other murids and organize a hadra, even if small — two or three gathered in remembrance is sufficient.

Q: What prophetic model does the hadra follow?
A: The assemblies (majalis) of the Prophet ﷺ — gatherings the companions described as "gardens of paradise."
```
