The **Ism al-Mufrad** is the fourth of the seven pillars. See \[\[Ism al-Mufrad]] in the Dictionary.

## Centrality

Shaykh al-Alawi, whose method the Karkariya inherits, considered the dhikr of the singular name *Allah* to be the crowning practice of the path. The Shaykh has continued this emphasis, describing it as the practice that "burns the veils" between the servant and the Lord.

## Transmission

The method of the Ism al-Mufrad is transmitted directly from Shaykh to murid. This transmission (*talqin*) is itself considered a spiritual act — the Shaykh breathes the name into the heart of the murid through a specific method, charging it with the baraka of the \[\[Silsila]].

## Without transmission

The Shaykh warns against practicing this dhikr without proper transmission and guidance. The concentrated power of the name, practiced incorrectly or without supervision, can lead to spiritual disturbance. The guide is the safety.
