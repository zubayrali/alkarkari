**Siyaha** (Arabic: سِيَاحَة, "wandering" or "traveling") is one of the seven foundational pillars of the Tariqa Karkariya. It refers to spiritual travel undertaken for the sake of God — visiting the Shaykh, attending hadras in distant places, or undertaking journeys of sacred pilgrimage and spiritual intent.

## Meaning

The root *s-y-h* suggests flowing water and wide movement. In the Sufi context, siyaha is the outward correlate of the inward journey: just as the heart must leave behind its attachments to travel toward God, the body may be called to leave behind familiar comfort.

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Travel, for you will be healthy and provided for." The Sufi tradition read this on multiple levels.

## Forms

* **Visiting the Shaykh** — the murid's journey to be in the physical presence of the guide
* **Attending gatherings** — traveling to a hadra or ziyara (visit to a saint's tomb)
* **Khalwa travel** — relocating to a new place for a period of spiritual retreat (\[\[Khalwa]])
* **Siyaha of the heart** — the inward dimension: detachment from the world's pull

## In the Karkariya today

Murids of the Karkariya travel to Morocco — particularly to Fes and the Shaykh's zawiya — for the major annual gatherings and to renew their connection. These journeys are considered a form of siyaha.
