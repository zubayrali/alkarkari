**Zikr** (also *dhikr*, Arabic: ذِكْر, "remembrance" or "mention") is the practice of remembering God through the repeated utterance of His names, attributes, or formulas of praise. It is the central practice of all Sufi orders and the engine of the spiritual life in the Karkariya.

## Quranic basis

> "Remember God with much remembrance" (Quran 33:41)

> "Verily in the remembrance of God do hearts find rest." (Quran 13:28)

The Quran commands zikr more frequently than almost any other act of worship. The Sufi tradition took this mandate seriously and developed elaborate sciences of its application.

## Forms in the Karkariya

| Form          | Description                                   |
| ------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Zikr al-lisan | Verbal remembrance with the tongue            |
| Zikr al-qalb  | Remembrance with the heart                    |
| Zikr al-sirr  | Remembrance in the \[\[Sirr]] — the innermost |
| Zikr al-ruh   | Remembrance of the spirit                     |
| Zikr al-khafi | The hidden remembrance                        |

## Individual and collective

The \[\[Wird]] is the individual daily zikr. The \[\[Hadra]] is the collective zikr. Both are essential — one trains the private relationship with God, the other the communal fabric of the Tariqa.

## The \[\[Ism al-Mufrad]]

The singular name *Allah* represents the summit of dhikr practice in the Karkariya method — the point where all formulas dissolve into pure presence.
