**Murid** (Arabic: مُريد, "one who desires" or "one who wills") is the term for a person who has formally entered the Tariqa through the act of initiation (\[\[Bay'a]]). The word derives from *irada* — will or intention — signifying that the murid has made a deliberate choice to travel the path under the guidance of a Shaykh.

## The murid's obligations

Upon taking bay'a, the murid undertakes:

* Daily recitation of the \[\[Wird]]
* Attendance at the \[\[Hadra]] when possible
* Obedience to the Shaykh's guidance (*ta'a*)
* Sincere repentance (*tawba*) and avoidance of major sins
* Regular spiritual accounting (*muhasaba*)

## The murid's relationship with the Shaykh

The Karkariya teaching places great emphasis on the inner connection (rabita) between murid and Shaykh. The Shaykh serves as a spiritual physician — diagnosing the diseases of the soul and prescribing remedies from the treasury of the Tariqa. The murid's trust (*tawakkul*) in this relationship is foundational.

> "The murid before his Shaykh is like the body in the hands of the washer." — classical Sufi saying

## Levels

Not all murids are at the same stage. The Tariqa distinguishes between the beginning murid (*mubtadi'*), the intermediate (*mutawassit*), and the advanced (*muntahi*) — though these are internal states, not formal ranks.
