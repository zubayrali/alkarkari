**Khalwa** (Arabic: خَلْوَة, "seclusion" or "solitude") is the practice of withdrawing from ordinary social life for a defined period to devote oneself entirely to worship, dhikr, and purification of the heart. It is one of the seven foundational pillars of the Tariqa Karkariya.

## Historical roots

Khalwa has roots in prophetic practice — the Prophet ﷺ would withdraw to the Cave of Hira before his prophethood. It became a central element of Sufi training across all major orders, including the Shadhiliyya lineage from which the Karkariya descends.

## The Karkariya khalwa

The Shaykh prescribes khalwa individually, based on the readiness and state of the murid. Key elements:

* A designated space (often the zawiya or a room prepared for the purpose)
* Minimal food and sleep
* Continuous dhikr, particularly the \[\[Ism al-Mufrad]]
* Recitation of the \[\[Wird]]
* Minimal speech with others
* Regular reporting to the Shaykh about visions and inner states

## Duration

Khalwas in the Karkariya may last from a few days to forty days (the traditional *arba'in*), depending on the murid's state and the Shaykh's guidance.

## Caution

The Shaykh emphasizes that khalwa undertaken without proper guidance or preparation is dangerous. The isolation can amplify not only spiritual openings but also the play of the ego and illusion. The presence of the Shaykh's guidance is essential.
