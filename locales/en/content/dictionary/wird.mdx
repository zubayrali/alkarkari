The **wird** (Arabic: وِرْد, pl. *awrad*) is the foundational daily litany assigned to every murid upon entry into the Tariqa. It consists of a sequence of formulas of remembrance (dhikr), prayers upon the Prophet (salawat), and Quranic recitations performed at fixed times — typically at dawn and after the Asr or Maghrib prayer.

## Structure

The wird of the Karkariya follows the classical Shadhili pattern, drawn from the lineage through Sidi Ahmad ibn Idris and Shaykh Ahmad al-Alawi. It includes:

* **Istighfar** — seeking forgiveness (100×)
* **Salat al-Fatih** — the opening prayer upon the Prophet
* **La ilaha illa Allah** — the declaration of divine unity (100×)
* **Salat al-Mashishiyya** — the prayer of Ibn Mashish

## Significance

The wird is considered the lifeline of the murid's connection to the Shaykh and, through him, to the Prophet ﷺ. Missing it without excuse is considered a serious spiritual lapse. The Shaykh has described it as "the rope between the servant and his Lord."
