## The Seven Pillars

![Books on a shelf](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1524995997946-a1c2e315a42f?w=800\&h=400\&fit=crop)

The Tariqa Karkariya is built upon **seven foundational practices**, each serving a distinct role in the spiritual journey of the *murid*.

> "Whoever does not have a wird has no warid."
> — Sufi maxim

## Wird — The Daily Litany

The first obligation of the murid and the foundation upon which all other practices rest.

The Wird consists of:

1. **Quranic verses** — selected by the Shaykh
2. **Prayers upon the Prophet** — salawat
3. **Divine names** — specific adhkar
4. **Supplication** — personal du'a

It is recited at *specific times* — typically after Fajr and after Maghrib.

## Hadra — The Gathering

![People gathering](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1529156069898-49953e39b3ac?w=800\&h=400\&fit=crop)

The collective spiritual gathering combining dhikr, poetry, and presence.

* Recitation of litanies
* Qasidas (spiritual poetry)
* Standing dhikr with movement
* Collective supplication

## Math & Formulas

The relationship between the pillars follows a pattern:

$$
\text{Completion} = \sum_{i=1}^{7} P_i \cdot \frac{1}{\text{depth}_i}
$$

Where each $P_i$ represents the degree of realization in each pillar.

## Nested Lists & Emphasis

### The Three Stages

* **Shari'a** (Law)
  * Outward compliance
  * The body's station
* **Tariqa** (Path)
  * Inward refinement
  * The heart's station
* **Haqiqa** (Reality)
  * Direct witnessing
  * The spirit's station

> The three are not sequential but simultaneous — like the shell, the kernel, and the oil of a nut.
