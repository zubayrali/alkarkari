**Abd al-Salam ibn Mashish** (Arabic: عبد السلام بن مشيش, d. 625 AH / 1228 CE) is one of the great poles (*aqtab*) of the Sufi tradition and the master of \[\[Abu al-Hasan al-Shadhili]], the founder of the Shadhiliyya from which the Karkariya ultimately descends.

## Life

Ibn Mashish lived as a recluse on Jabal 'Alam in northern Morocco (near present-day Tetouan), a mountain that became a place of pilgrimage. He was known for extraordinary spiritual states and for receiving visitors from across the Islamic world who sought his blessing and guidance.

## The famous prayer

Ibn Mashish composed the **Salat al-Mashishiyya** (صلاة المشيشية), one of the most celebrated prayers upon the Prophet ﷺ in Islamic history, which remains part of the spiritual repertoire of the Karkariya and countless other paths. Its opening lines describe the Prophet as the ocean of divine lights from which all existence flows.

> "O God, send blessings upon him from whom the secrets burst forth and the lights overflow\..."

## Encounter with al-Shadhili

When Abu al-Hasan al-Shadhili came to Ibn Mashish seeking a master, he was instructed to first ritually wash himself of all worldly knowledge. After this symbolic purification, Ibn Mashish took his hand and gave him the wird, saying: "Now you are ready."

## His tomb

The tomb of Ibn Mashish on Jabal 'Alam is a major site of *ziyara* (visitation) in Morocco, visited by hundreds of thousands annually.
