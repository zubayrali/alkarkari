**Abu al-Hasan Ali ibn Abdallah al-Shadhili** (Arabic: أبو الحسن الشاذلي, c. 593–656 AH / 1196–1258 CE) was a Moroccan-born Sufi master who founded the **Shadhiliyya** order — the vast family of Sufi paths from which the Karkariya descends.

## Life

Born near Ceuta (in present-day northern Morocco), al-Shadhili traveled extensively in search of a master, eventually finding his guide in \[\[Abd al-Salam ibn Mashish]] on Jabal 'Alam. After his initiation, he journeyed to Ifriqiya (Tunisia) and then settled in Alexandria, Egypt, where he gathered a major circle of disciples.

He died during the Hajj pilgrimage at Humaythara in the Egyptian desert in 1258 CE.

## Teaching style

Al-Shadhili was famous for teaching in the marketplace, not in isolated retreats. He emphasized that the Sufi path was compatible with ordinary professional and family life — that the inward journey did not require withdrawal from the world. This teaching shaped the entire Shadhili tradition.

> "Do not carry the burdens of the world, for this is the Lord's business. Your duty is the path."

## The Shadhili method

Key emphases of al-Shadhili's method that carry through to the Karkariya:

* Trust in God (*tawakkul*) without abandoning worldly work
* The importance of the \[\[Wird]] as daily foundation
* Sincere repentance and moral rectification before mystical aspiration
* The centrality of the Prophet ﷺ as model and intermediary
