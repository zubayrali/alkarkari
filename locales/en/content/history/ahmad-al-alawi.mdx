**Shaykh Ahmad ibn Mustafa al-Alawi** (Arabic: أحمد العلوي, 1869–1934 CE) was an Algerian Sufi master of Moroccan descent, founder of the **Alawiyya** order and one of the most influential Islamic figures of the twentieth century. The Tariqa Karkariya inherits its method most directly through him.

## Life

Born in Mostaganem, Algeria, al-Alawi first worked as a cobbler and practiced various folk religious traditions before encountering the Shadhili-Darqawi path under Shaykh Muhammad al-Buzidi, who introduced him to the dhikr of the \[\[Ism al-Mufrad]]. This encounter transformed him. After al-Buzidi's death, al-Alawi became an independent master and founded the Alawiyya, which spread across North and West Africa, the Middle East, and eventually to European Muslim communities.

## Influence on the Karkariya

The Karkariya's distinctive emphasis on the \[\[Ism al-Mufrad]], the method of \[\[Muraqqa']], and the structure of the seven pillars draws directly from the Alawiyya tradition. Shaykh Mohamed Faouzi al-Karkari has described al-Alawi as "the greatest Shaykh of our age."

## European encounter

Al-Alawi was one of the first Sufi masters to have significant contact with European seekers. The French scholar Martin Lings (Abu Bakr Siraj al-Din) wrote an influential biography of him: *A Sufi Saint of the Twentieth Century*.

## Written works

Al-Alawi was a prolific writer. His major works include:

* *Minah al-Quddusiyya* — on Sufi stations
* *Al-Qawl al-Ma'ruf* — responses to critics
* *Diwan* — collected poetry
