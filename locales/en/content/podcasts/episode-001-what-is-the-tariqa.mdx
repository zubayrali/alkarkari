## Transcript excerpt

**Host:** We're starting from the beginning today. For someone who has never heard of the Tariqa Karkariya, what would you say it is?

**Guest:** It's a Sufi path — a school of the heart. The word "tariqa" itself means a way or a road. It's a school for traveling toward God, with a living guide who has himself traveled the road and can help you navigate it.

**Host:** Why do you need a guide? Can't you just read books?

**Guest:** You can read all the maps in the world and still get lost. The guide isn't a teacher in the academic sense — he's more like a doctor. He can see things in your inner state that you can't see yourself, because he's been through those stages and because he has a specific kind of perception that comes from his own realization.

**Host:** How does one enter?

**Guest:** Through the [baya](../dictionary/baya.mdx) — the pledge. You come to the Shaykh, or to one of his representatives, and you take his hand and make a commitment. You receive the \[\[Wird]]. And then you begin.

**Host:** Is it a big commitment?

**Guest:** It's a lifelong commitment — but it begins small. Daily wird, attendance at the \[\[Hadra]] when you can. The Shaykh meets you where you are.
