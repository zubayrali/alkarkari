The **Tariqa Karkariya** is a contemporary Sufi order rooted in the **Shadhili-Alawi** tradition of North Africa. It was established by \[\[Shaykh Mohamed Faouzi al-Karkari]] in Fes, Morocco, in the late twentieth century.

## Lineage summary

The Karkariya's roots reach back through a chain of masters:

| Master                                 | Period    | Role                       |
| -------------------------------------- | --------- | -------------------------- |
| \[\[Abu al-Hasan al-Shadhili]]         | 1196–1258 | Founder of the Shadhiliyya |
| Abu al-Abbas al-Mursi                  | 1219–1287 | First successor            |
| Ibn Ata Allah al-Iskandari             | d. 1309   | Author, systematizer       |
| ...                                    | ...       | Chain continues            |
| Sidi Ahmad ibn Idris al-Fasi           | 1760–1837 | Major reformer             |
| \[\[Ahmad al-Alawi]]                   | 1869–1934 | Alawiyya branch            |
| \[\[Shaykh Mohamed Faouzi al-Karkari]] | b. 1974   | Present guide              |

## What makes it distinct

Within the broad Shadhili family, the Karkariya is characterized by:

1. **Centrality of the \[\[Ism al-Mufrad]]** — inherited directly from the Alawiyya
2. **The seven-pillar framework** — a clear pedagogical structure for the path
3. **Openness to global murids** — active outreach to Western seekers
4. **Contemporary language** — the Shaykh translates classical teaching into modern terms without diluting it

## Fes as spiritual home

The Tariqa is centered in **Fes**, Morocco — a city with an unbroken tradition of Islamic scholarship and Sufi life since the ninth century. The city is home to the Qarawiyyin mosque-university (founded 859 CE), numerous zawiyas, and the shrines of great Moroccan saints.
