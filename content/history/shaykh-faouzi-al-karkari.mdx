**Shaykh Mohamed Faouzi al-Karkari** (Arabic: محمد فوزي الكركري) was born in **Fes, Morocco in 1974** and is the present guide (*murshid*) of the Tariqa Karkariya. He received his spiritual formation within the Shadhili-Alawi tradition and was designated as a master by his own guide before establishing the Karkariya as a distinct expression of that lineage.

## Background

Shaykh al-Karkari was raised in Fes, the spiritual capital of Morocco and home of the zawiya of Moulay Idris, the tomb of \[\[Abd al-Salam ibn Mashish]]'s spiritual descendants, and centuries of continuous Sufi life. He memorized the Quran and received a traditional Islamic education alongside his Sufi formation.

## The name "Karkariya"

The name derives from *karkara* — a sound of flowing water or gentle movement — reflecting the Shaykh's teaching that the path moves like water: without forcing, finding the natural channel, wearing away obstacles gradually.

## Teaching approach

The Shaykh is known for:

* Accessibility to murids across the world, including Western converts
* Emphasis on the compatibility of the path with ordinary professional life
* Deep engagement with classical Sufi texts alongside oral transmission
* A warm and direct teaching style that makes difficult spiritual concepts approachable

## Global community

Through travel and the internet, the Karkariya has gathered murids across Morocco, Europe, North America, and beyond. The Shaykh visits murids in their home countries and holds major gatherings in Fes.

## Published works

The Shaykh has authored and authorized several works on the Karkariya method, including collections of his teachings and explanations of the \[\[Wird]] and the seven pillars.
