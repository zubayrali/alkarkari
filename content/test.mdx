## Citations inside Sidenotes (GFM footnotes)

Aesthetic realism holds that values exist independently of human responses — aesthetic properties are objective features objects genuinely possess[^1].

Anti-realism counters that values are constituted by ideal responses[^2]. Fitting attitude theories attempt a middle path: something has aesthetic value if it merits particular responses — admiration, contemplation, delight [@scanlon1998what].

[^1]: Mackie's challenge: values would be "metaphysically queer" — objectively prescriptive, motivating by their mere apprehension. Would require a special faculty of aesthetic perception. "To-be-appreciated-ness somehow built into" the property [@mackie1977ethics].

[^2]: The problem is explaining normativity. If beauty just is whatever produces pleasure in ideal observers, why should non-ideal observers care? Why does "this is beautiful" make a normative claim on us?
