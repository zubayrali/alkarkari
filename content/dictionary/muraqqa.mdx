**Muraqqa'** (Arabic: مُرَاقَبَة, *muraqaba*) means watchfulness, vigilance, or contemplative awareness. In the Karkariya, it is one of the seven foundational pillars and refers to the sustained inward attention of the heart to the divine presence.

## Meaning

The root *r-q-b* carries the sense of watching over, guarding, and being observed. Muraqaba is the practice of being aware that one is always under divine observation — and of orienting the heart continuously toward that reality.

> "Worship Allah as though you see Him; if you do not see Him, He sees you." — Hadith Jibril

## Practice

Muraqaba in the Karkariya is not passive. It involves:

* Sitting in stillness with closed eyes
* Directing the heart toward a specific divine name or attribute, as instructed by the Shaykh
* Observing thoughts, images, and inner states without attachment
* Sustaining the connection (rabita) with the Shaykh inwardly

Advanced muraqaba may involve specific contemplations (mushahada) assigned individually by the Shaykh.

## Relation to other practices

Muraqaba deepens the fruits of the \[\[Wird]] and \[\[Hadra]]. Where dhikr moves the tongue and heart, muraqaba stills both to receive what dhikr has opened.
