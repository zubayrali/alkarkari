**Bay'a** (Arabic: بَيْعَة, "pledge" or "allegiance") is the formal act of initiation into the Tariqa Karkariya, in which the aspirant takes the hand of the Shaykh (or his representative) and pledges to follow the path under his guidance.

## Meaning

The word *bay'a* is the same used for the historical pledge of allegiance to the Prophet ﷺ. In the hadith of the tree (bay'at al-ridwan), companions pledged with the Prophet under a tree before the treaty of Hudaybiya. The Sufi bay'a is understood as a continuation of this covenant — each bay'a in the chain connecting back to that original pledge.

## What happens in bay'a

1. The aspirant states their intention
2. The Shaykh (or authorized representative) takes their hand
3. The Fatiha is recited
4. The Shaykh breathes the wird formula (or its transmission) into the murid's heart
5. The murid receives the \[\[Wird]] and instructions

## Who can give bay'a

In the Karkariya, bay'a is given by Shaykh Mohamed Faouzi al-Karkari or by authorized representatives (*muqaddams*) who have been granted this permission. Bay'a given by an unauthorized person is considered without effect.

## Renewal

Bay'a can be renewed — particularly when a murid visits the Shaykh in person — deepening the initial connection.
