The **hadra** (Arabic: حَضْرَة, "presence") is the communal gathering of the Tariqa in which murids collectively perform dhikr, recite poetry in praise of the Prophet ﷺ, and enter states of spiritual presence (hudur). It is one of the seven foundational pillars of the Karkariya.

## Form

The hadra typically unfolds in stages:

1. **Opening recitations** — Fatiha and salawat
2. **Collective dhikr** — rhythmic remembrance, beginning slow and increasing in tempo
3. **Inshad** — devotional poetry (qasaid), often from the Burda, Hamziyya, or compositions of the Shaykh
4. **Hal** — the spiritual states that may arise among participants

## Setting

Hadras are held in the zawiya or designated meeting spaces. The Shaykh or a designated representative (muqaddam) leads the gathering. Men and women may gather separately or in designated arrangements depending on local custom.

## Inner meaning

The word *hadra* itself points to the purpose: the "presence" refers not only to the gathering of people but to the sought-for spiritual presence of the Prophet ﷺ and, ultimately, the divine presence. The Shaykh teaches that the hadra is a school of the heart, not merely a ritual.
