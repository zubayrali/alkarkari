**Silsila** (Arabic: سِلسِلَة, "chain") is the unbroken chain of spiritual transmission linking each master of the Tariqa to his predecessor, and ultimately to the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. It is the guarantee of the authenticity and living power of the path.

## The Karkariya silsila

The Tariqa Karkariya traces its lineage through the following principal links:

1. **The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ**
2. **Sayyidna Ali ibn Abi Talib** (and via Abu Bakr al-Siddiq)
3. ... *(continuous chain through the centuries)*
4. **Abd al-Salam ibn Mashish** (d. 1228) — the Moroccan pole
5. **Abu al-Hasan al-Shadhili** (d. 1258) — founder of the Shadhiliyya
6. **Abu al-Abbas al-Mursi** (d. 1287)
7. **Ibn Ata Allah al-Iskandari** (d. 1309)
8. ... *(Shadhili chain continues)*
9. **Sidi Ahmad ibn Idris al-Fasi** (d. 1837)
10. **Sidi Muhammad al-Fasi**
11. **Shaykh Ahmad al-Alawi** (d. 1934) — via the Alawiyya branch
12. **Shaykh Mohamed Faouzi al-Karkari** (b. 1974) — present guide of the Karkariya

## Significance

The silsila is not merely biographical. In the Sufi understanding, the baraka (spiritual blessing) of the Prophet ﷺ flows through these human channels like light through a chain of lamps. Taking the hand of the Shaykh (*bay'a*) connects the murid to this living current.
