**Ism al-Mufrad** (Arabic: اسم المُفرَد, "the Singular Name") refers to the dhikr of the divine name **Allah** (الله) alone — not as a phrase, but as a single name repeated with full presence of heart. It is one of the seven foundational pillars of the Tariqa Karkariya.

## Distinction from other dhikr

Where the formula *La ilaha illa Allah* (the negation-affirmation) is the dhikr of the \[\[Wird]], the Ism al-Mufrad is a more concentrated practice. It consists solely of the name **Allah**, breathed or spoken inwardly with each breath, until the name occupies the whole of the inner space.

Shaykh al-Alawi, a major influence on the Karkariya's method, considered this dhikr the summit of the tariqas he had encountered.

## Method

The murid is instructed in the proper technique by the Shaykh or his representative. Generally:

* The name is pronounced with the lips, then increasingly with the heart alone
* Breath is coordinated with the name
* The gaze of the heart (basar al-qalb) is directed toward the meaning, not the letters

## Significance

The Ism al-Mufrad is considered the most direct path to fana' (annihilation) and baqa' (subsistence in God). The Shaykh has described it as "the key that opens the lock of the heart."
