## Transcript excerpt

**Host:** Today we're going through the seven pillars. Let's start with the wird. What is it, and why is it the foundation?

**Guest:** The wird is the daily litany — a specific sequence of remembrance formulas that you recite morning and evening. It's the foundation because it's what keeps the connection alive day by day. The Shaykh describes it as the rope between the murid and his Lord.

**Host:** It sounds simple.

**Guest:** It is simple. That's the point. Consistency beats complexity every time. A short wird done every single day for twenty years will transform a person completely. An elaborate practice done once a week won't.

**Host:** What about the hadra — the gathering?

**Guest:** The hadra is where individual practice becomes communal. When people gather in remembrance, something happens that can't happen alone. There's a collective elevation. You feel it even if you can't explain it.

**Host:** And the Ism al-Mufrad — the singular name?

**Guest:** This is the crown of the method. The name *Allah* — just the name, nothing else. You practice it until it fills the whole interior. Shaykh al-Alawi described it as the key that unlocks everything. The Shaykh has continued that emphasis.

**Host:** What about khalwa — the retreat? Is that for everyone?

**Guest:** Not immediately. The Shaykh gives khalwa when he judges a murid is ready. It requires a stable foundation in the earlier pillars. But it's available to everyone — it's not reserved for specialists or full-time students.
