**Kitab al-Hikam** (كتاب الحكم, "The Book of Wisdoms") is a collection of aphorisms by **Ibn Ata Allah al-Iskandari** (d. 1309 CE), the third master in the \[\[Silsila]] after \[\[Abu al-Hasan al-Shadhili]] and Abu al-Abbas al-Mursi.

## Character

The *Hikam* consists of 264 short aphorisms — some a single sentence, some a short paragraph — covering the full range of the Sufi path: repentance, reliance on God, the nature of the ego, the secrets of divine action, the relationship between master and disciple.

It has been commented upon by virtually every major Sufi master since its composition and is studied in the Karkariya.

## Selected aphorisms

> "Your desire that people should know your particular virtue is proof that you have not been sincere toward God in your acts."

> "Nothing in creation is more astonishing than the one who knows God's all-encompassing mercy and then despairs."

> "The light of the heart is better than the light of the intellect, just as the sun is better than the lamp."

## Editions in English

Multiple English translations exist, including by Victor Danner and by Ibn Abbad al-Rundi (commentary edition).
