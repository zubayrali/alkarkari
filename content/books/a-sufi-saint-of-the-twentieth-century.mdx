**A Sufi Saint of the Twentieth Century** is a biography of \[\[Ahmad al-Alawi]] written by Martin Lings (1909–2005), a British scholar and Sufi who became a student of the Alawiyya tradition. Published in 1961, it remains the most comprehensive account in English of al-Alawi's life and teaching.

## Contents

The book covers:

* Al-Alawi's early life in Mostaganem
* His encounter with the \[\[Ism al-Mufrad]] under Shaykh al-Buzidi
* The founding of the Alawiyya and its spread
* Encounters with European visitors and scholars
* Al-Alawi's written works and poetry
* Doctrinal expositions of key Sufi concepts

## Significance for Karkariya murids

Because the Karkariya inherits its method so directly from al-Alawi, this book serves as essential background reading. Understanding al-Alawi's teaching context clarifies many aspects of the Karkariya method.
