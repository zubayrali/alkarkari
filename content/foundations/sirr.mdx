The **Sirr** is the seventh and culminating pillar of the Tariqa Karkariya. See \[\[Sirr]] in the Dictionary.

## The goal of the path

If the earlier six pillars are the ladder, the sirr is what is reached at the top. The entire architecture of the Karkariya method — wird, hadra, muraqaba, ism al-mufrad, siyaha, khalwa — serves the activation and purification of the sirr, the innermost faculty where the divine disclosure (*kashf*) and intimate witnessing (*mushahada*) occur.

## Guarding the sirr

One of the Shaykh's most consistent teachings is the *guarding of the sirr* — the practice of not speaking freely about one's spiritual experiences, visions, and states. The sirr is not to be displayed. When it is exposed, it leaks and diminishes.

> "The secret that is spoken is lost." — Karkariya teaching

## The sirr and the Shaykh

There is a dimension of the sirr that belongs to the relationship between murid and Shaykh. The Shaykh has access to the murid's inner state in a way that the murid themselves may not fully understand. This is why the relationship is built on trust and why the murid must be honest with the Shaykh even when it is difficult.

## Completion

The seven pillars are not sequential stages that are left behind. They are simultaneous dimensions of the path — each feeding the others, all serving the emergence of the sirr into its fullness.
