The **Muraqqa'** is the third of the seven pillars. See \[\[Muraqqa']] in the Dictionary.

## Muraqqa' as a daily practice

Unlike the \[\[Wird]] which has fixed times, muraqaba is ideally a continuous state that is also practiced in formal sitting. The Shaykh prescribes dedicated times for muraqaba — typically after Fajr and after 'Isha — but the deeper aspiration is that the state of inner watchfulness become unbroken.

## The inner teacher

The Shaykh teaches that muraqaba, properly practiced, activates an inner guidance that begins to correct the murid from within. Rather than always needing external instruction, the murid starts to feel a pull away from what harms the heart and toward what nourishes it. This is the beginning of the *kashf* (unveiling).

## Stages

1. **Muraqabat al-af'al** — watchfulness of actions
2. **Muraqabat al-ahwal** — watchfulness of states
3. **Muraqabat al-qalb** — watchfulness of the heart's movements
4. **Muraqabat al-sirr** — watchfulness in the \[\[Sirr]]
