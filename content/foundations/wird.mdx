The **Wird** is the first of the seven pillars of the Tariqa Karkariya. See \[\[Wird]] in the Dictionary for the full definition.

## The wird as pillar

The Shaykh has described the wird as the foundation of the house — without it, nothing stands. Every other practice (hadra, khalwa, siyaha) rests on the daily habit of the wird.

A murid who abandons the wird has, in effect, stepped off the path. The Shaykh distinguishes between missing it through genuine unavoidable circumstance (travel, illness) — for which there is latitude — and abandoning it through negligence, which severs the inner connection.

## Consistency over intensity

The Karkariya teaching emphasizes consistency over the intensity of any single sitting. A brief wird performed daily is worth more than an extraordinary session followed by weeks of absence. This principle — *al-istimrar* (continuity) — runs through all the pillars.
