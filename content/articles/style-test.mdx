## Inline annotations

Here is a ==highlighted phrase== inside a sentence. This one is !!underlined by hand!!, and this is ^^boxed in orange^^. The engine can also draw a ((circle)) around words, or put ||brackets around a phrase|| like an editor's note.

Two in one line: ==first mark== and then ((second mark)) right after.

An unclosed ==delimiter should render as plain text without breaking anything.

## Annotations in headings are ==ignored==

The heading above must render its `==` literally — annotations are skipped inside headings.

## Sidenotes

Standard footnotes become margin sidenotes[^1] on wide screens, and collapse to click-to-expand inline notes on narrow ones. A second reference[^long] tests multi-paragraph footnote content, and the same note can even be referenced twice[^1].

The \[\[Wird]] is recited daily.[^wikilink]

[^1]: A short sidenote. It should appear in the margin next to its reference number.

[^long]: A longer footnote with two paragraphs.

    The second paragraph should render as a separate block inside the sidenote, not break the page layout.

[^wikilink]: Sidenotes can contain *formatting* and `code` too.
